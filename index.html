01 — Why Calgary

Two Reasons Calgary
Leads the Country

REASON 01 — ENERGY CAPITAL

Canada's Oil & Gas Headquarters

Calgary is home to more oil and gas company headquarters than any other city in Canada. The city sits at the centre of Alberta's oil sands industry — one of the largest hydrocarbon deposits ever discovered. With over 170 billion barrels of proven reserves, Alberta is rivalled only by Saudi Arabia and Venezuela in the global energy landscape.

The University of Calgary, SAIT, and Mount Royal University produce thousands of graduates each year specifically trained for the petroleum, geoscience, and engineering sectors — giving any incoming company immediate access to a deep, specialized talent pool.

REASON 02 — BUSINESS ENVIRONMENT

The Most Business-Friendly Province in Canada

Alberta stands apart from every other Canadian province when it comes to operating costs. There is no provincial sales tax — the only province in Canada with this distinction — which directly reduces the cost of goods, services, and operations. Alberta's corporate tax rate is among the lowest in Canada, and the province actively recruits foreign investment through dedicated government programs.

Compared to Toronto or Vancouver, Calgary offers dramatically lower commercial real estate costs, lower labour costs for equivalent talent, and a government that treats the private sector as a partner.

Compared to Houston, Abu Dhabi, or Toronto — Calgary offers a unique combination of resource access, business costs, and political stability that no other energy hub can match.

Resource Depth
Alberta's oil sands represent one of the most resource-rich jurisdictions on the planet. 05 — Tailored for Aramco

Why Calgary
for Aramco Ventures?

Aramco Ventures is Saudi Aramco's venture capital and strategic investment arm, focused on identifying and scaling the next generation of energy technologies. With capital deployed across the Middle East, Asia, and Europe — North America remains a notable gap.

Calgary is the only city in the world where Aramco's core expertise — heavy oil production, pipeline logistics, large-scale industrial project management — is not just relevant, but deeply embedded in the local economy.

"Both Saudi Arabia and Alberta built their economies on oil. Calgary is the only city in the world where Aramco's expertise is not just relevant — it's essential." — Calgary Economic Development Office
🛢️
Shared DNA — Oil Economies Diversifying
Saudi Arabia and Alberta are two of the world's most oil-dependent economies — and both are actively diversifying into clean energy, hydrogen, and carbon capture. Aramco's Vision 2030 strategy mirrors exactly what Alberta's energy transition looks like. An investment in Calgary isn't just oil — it's a blueprint for how Aramco can learn from Canada's clean energy transition and apply those lessons globally.
🌍
Zero North American Presence — First Mover Advantage
Aramco Ventures has no Canadian operations. Calgary represents untapped territory with established deal flow and no conflict of interest. Being the first major Middle Eastern energy investment vehicle to establish a Calgary base gives Aramco preferential access to deals before competitors like ADNOC Ventures or Kuwait Petroleum enter the market.
📈
Invest in What You Know
Oil sands extraction, pipeline infrastructure, heavy crude processing, and large-scale industrial project management are Aramco's core competencies. These are also exactly the industries that define Calgary's economy. Unlike investing in unfamiliar tech sectors, Calgary gives Aramco a home-field advantage in an industry it understands better than anyone.
🤝
Strategic Fit with Vision 2030
Saudi Vision 2030 calls for Aramco to diversify revenue sources, develop international partnerships, and invest in next-generation energy technologies. A Calgary office delivers on all three simultaneously — opening North American revenue streams, building partnerships with Suncor and Enbridge, and positioning Aramco inside Canada's clean energy innovation corridor.
